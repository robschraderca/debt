The Reality

Most people don't know this about collection accounts

The debt collection industry is built on volume, not accuracy. Debts get sold and resold, records get lost, balances get inflated. By the time a collector contacts you, there's a good chance something is wrong with what they're claiming.

Documentation Gets Lost

Original account records often don't survive the buying and selling of debt. Without proper documentation, collectors may have no legal right to collect.

Time Limits Expire

Every debt has a statute of limitations. Once it expires, collectors lose their ability to sue — but many try to collect anyway.

Balances Get Inflated

Unauthorized fees, incorrect interest calculations, and duplicate charges are common. You may owe far less than they claim — or nothing at all.

Wrong Person Targeted

Identity mix-ups happen constantly. Debts get assigned to the wrong person, or to someone who was only an authorized user, not the account holder.

Know Your Rights

Federal law is on your side

You have powerful legal protections that most people don't know about. When collectors violate these laws, you may be entitled to damages — and the debt itself may become uncollectable.

What the law guarantees you

  • Right to written proof Collectors must provide documentation proving the debt is valid, accurate, and legally collectible from you specifically.
  • Freedom from harassment No threats, no abuse, no calls before 8am or after 9pm, no contacting your workplace. Violations can result in damages paid to you.
  • Accurate credit reporting Credit bureaus must investigate disputes and remove any information that cannot be verified as accurate and complete.
  • Protection from expired debts Once the statute of limitations passes, collectors lose their ability to sue. Continuing to pursue payment may itself be a violation.

The Laws We Use

FDCPA

Prohibits harassment, threats, and deceptive practices. Violations can mean $1,000+ in statutory damages.

FCRA

Requires accurate credit reporting. Bureaus must investigate and remove unverifiable information.

TCPA

Restricts robocalls and autodialed calls. Violations can result in $500-$1,500 per call.
