Need to Know

Practical Information

üöó Getting There

1 hour from LIR Liberia airport. Rent a car ($45-70/day) or take a shared shuttle ($40-50). Private transfer ~$100.

üí∞ Money

USD widely accepted. Colones for small purchases. ATMs in town center. Credit cards at most businesses.

üè• Healthcare

Multiple clinics in town. Hospital in Liberia (1 hour). Several pharmacies available. Travel insurance recommended.

üì± Connectivity

Good 4G/LTE coverage. Most cafes have WiFi. Consider Kolbi prepaid SIM ($10-15/month unlimited).

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety

Generally safe. Watch for strong currents. Don't leave valuables on beach or in cars. Use hotel safes.

üåßÔ∏è Best Time to Visit

Dry season Dec-Apr (hot, sunny). Green season May-Nov has fewer crowds, lower prices, occasional rain.

Local Secrets

Insider Tips

Local knowledge that makes the difference between tourist and traveler.

üèÑ Best surf is early morning (5-7am) before the wind. Witch's Rock and Ollie's Point are worth the boat trip for intermediates+.

üåÖ For sunset, skip crowded Tamarindo Beach. Head to Playa Langosta or take the estuary boat to Playa Grande for unobstructed views.

üê¢ Turtle nesting season runs Oct-Feb. Book Playa Grande tours in advance‚Äîlimited spots due to conservation rules.

üçΩÔ∏è Skip the beachfront restaurants for dinner. The best food is one block back‚Äîbetter prices, same quality.

üöó Rent a 4x4 if visiting during green season. Some roads flood. Reserve early in high season‚Äîcars sell out.

üõí Saturday morning farmer's market at the central park has the freshest produce and local goods. Arrive early.

üç∫ Happy hour runs 4-6pm at most bars. Sharky's and Crazy Monkey have the best sunset drink specials.

üèä The estuary between Tamarindo and Playa Grande has crocodiles. Don't swim across‚Äîtake the boat ($2).
