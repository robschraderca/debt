Understanding Your Options

What bankruptcy actually is—and isn't

Bankruptcy is a legal process that gives people overwhelmed by debt a chance to either eliminate most debts entirely or create a manageable repayment plan. It's not a moral judgment—it's a practical solution that exists precisely because life can throw curveballs no one sees coming.

Medical emergencies, job loss, divorce, business failures—these things happen to responsible, hardworking people every day. Bankruptcy was designed with this reality in mind. It's been part of U.S. law since the Constitution was written because the founders understood that second chances matter.

You're in good company. Over 400,000 Americans file for bankruptcy each year. Many go on to rebuild their credit and financial lives successfully within 2-3 years.

Types of Bankruptcy

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13: Which fits your situation?

Most individuals file under one of two chapters. The right choice depends on your income, assets, and goals.

Chapter 7

Liquidation Bankruptcy

"Clean slate" approach

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts—credit cards, medical bills, personal loans—typically within 3-4 months. In exchange, non-exempt assets may be sold to pay creditors, though most filers keep everything they own.

EligibilityMust pass means test
Timeline3-6 months
Debts dischargedMost unsecured debts
Asset impactMay lose non-exempt property
Credit report10 years
Best forLower income, few assets
Chapter 13

Reorganization Bankruptcy

"Catch up" approach

Chapter 13 lets you keep your assets while repaying debts over 3-5 years through a court-approved plan. It's especially useful for catching up on mortgage or car payments you've fallen behind on.

EligibilityRegular income required
Timeline3-5 years
Debts dischargedRemaining balance after plan
Asset impactKeep all property
Credit report7 years
Best forSteady income, protecting assets

Setting the Record Straight

What you've heard vs. what's actually true

Bankruptcy carries a lot of stigma and misinformation. Let's separate fact from fiction.

Myth

"I'll lose everything I own."

Reality

Most Chapter 7 filers keep all their property. Federal and state exemptions protect your home equity, car, retirement accounts, and personal belongings.

Myth

"My credit will be ruined forever."

Reality

Many people see credit score improvements within 12-18 months. By eliminating debt, you improve your debt-to-income ratio. Most can qualify for credit cards and car loans within 1-2 years.

Myth

"Everyone will know I filed."

Reality

While bankruptcy is public record, it's not announced in newspapers. Unless someone specifically searches federal court records, they won't know.

Myth

"Only irresponsible people file bankruptcy."

Reality

The vast majority of bankruptcies stem from medical debt, job loss, or divorce—not overspending. Most filers are middle-class people hit by circumstances beyond their control.
