The Challenge

When every bill has a different due date, rate, and minimumit's exhausting.

You're not bad with money. You're just drowning in logistics. Four cards, three due dates, interest rates you can barely track. Every month feels like a juggling act where one missed catch sends everything tumbling.

Consolidation isn't about hiding from debtit's about organizing it into something you can actually manage and pay off.

  • One payment, one due date, one rate
  • Often a lower overall interest rate
  • A clear payoff date you can count down to
  • Less mental load, more breathing room
Your Options

There's more than one way to consolidate. Here's what we work with.

The right approach depends on your credit, your debt amount, and what you're comfortable with. We'll help you understand which path makes the most sense.

Debt Consolidation Loan

A personal loan used specifically to pay off multiple debts at once, leaving you with one fixed monthly payment at (ideally) a lower rate.

  • Fixed rate and payment
  • Clear payoff timeline
  • Works for various credit profiles

Balance Transfer

Move high-interest credit card debt to a card with a 0% introductory APR. You pay down principal faster during the promo period.

  • 0% interest for 12-21 months
  • Best for good credit scores
  • No new loan required

Debt Management Plan

Work with a credit counseling agency who negotiates lower rates with your creditors. You make one payment to them; they distribute to creditors.

  • Reduced interest rates
  • No new credit required
  • Built-in financial coaching

Is This Right For You?

Consolidation works well for some situationsnot all. Here's how to know.

Consolidation might be right if...

  • You're juggling multiple debts with different rates and due dates
  • You can commit to not adding new debt during payoff
  • You'd qualify for a lower interest rate than you're currently paying
  • You have steady income to make consistent payments
  • The mental simplification alone would help you stay on track

Another approach might work better if...

  • You're not sure the debt itself is valid (consider Financial Restoration first)
  • Your income is unstable or you're between jobs
  • You wouldn't qualify for a better rate than you have now
  • The total debt is so overwhelming that consolidation won't solve it
